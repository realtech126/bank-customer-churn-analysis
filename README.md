# Bank Customer Churn Analysis
## Overview

Customer churn is a major challenge for banks because losing customers reduces long term revenue and increases customer acquisition costs. This project analyzes bank customer data to identify patterns that influence churn and provide insights that can help improve customer retention.

## Project Goal

Analyze customer data to identify the key factors that lead to customer churn and provide data driven insights that help the bank reduce customer loss.

## Objectives

• Calculate the overall customer churn rate

• Identify customer segments with high churn rates

• Examine the impact of credit score on churn

• Analyze the relationship between account balance and churn

• Evaluate how the number of bank products influences customer retention

• Compare churn behavior between active and inactive customers

## Dataset

The dataset contains information about bank customers and whether they exited the bank.

Each record represents a customer and includes demographic information, financial attributes, and account activity indicators.

Key variables in the dataset include

• CreditScore

• Geography

• Gender

• Age

• Tenure

• Balance

• NumberOfProducts

• HasCrCard

• IsActiveMember

• EstimatedSalary

• Exited which indicates if the customer left the bank

## Tools Used

• Excel for data cleaning and exploratory analysis

• Pivot tables and charts for analysis

• Data visualization to identify churn patterns

## Analysis Performed

• Customer churn rate analysis

• Customer segmentation by age, gender, and geography

• Product usage analysis

• Active vs inactive customer comparison

• Balance and credit score impact on churn

## Key Insights

• Customers with only one bank product show higher churn rates

• Inactive customers churn more frequently than active customers

• Certain geographic regions show higher churn levels

• Customers with higher balances sometimes move to competitors

• Product usage appears to improve customer retention

## Conclusion

Customer churn is influenced by customer engagement, product usage, and demographic characteristics. Customers who interact more with the bank and use multiple banking services are less likely to leave. Identifying these patterns helps the bank focus on customer groups that require retention strategies.

## Recommendations

• Encourage customers to adopt multiple banking products

• Improve engagement strategies for inactive customers

• Offer loyalty incentives to high value customers

• Monitor churn indicators regularly through dashboards
